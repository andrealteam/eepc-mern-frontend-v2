Shri Piyush Goyal

Hon'ble Minister of Commerce & Industry, Government of India

I am delighted to learn that the Engineering Export Promotion Council of India (EEPC) is celebrating its Platinum Jubilee on 8" September 2025 at Vigyan Bhawan, New Delhi. This remarkable milestone of unwavering dedication and service stands as a testament to EEPC’s profound contributions in shaping India's engineering export landscape.

Since its inception, EEPC has been a guiding beacon for India's engineering sector, relentlessly working to enhance the global competitiveness of Indian manufacturers. From facilitating policy advocacy to offering invaluable support to businesses, particularly SMEs, EEPC has played a pivotal role in empowering the industry to scale new heights. Furthermore, its leadership in organizing international exhibitions, buyer-seller meets and sourcing shows has not only showcased India's engineering capabilities to the world but also attracted global partnerships that have strengthened India’s position on the global trade map.

EEPC'’s relentless pursuit of excellence aligns perfectly with our Hon'ble Prime Minister Shri Narendra Modi ji's vision of a Viksit Bharat by 2047. As we mark this significant seven-decade journey, we celebrate not only EEPCs remarkable achievements but also its unwavering vision for the future, which will continue to inspire generations to come.

I extend my heartfelt congratulations to EEPC for this extraordinary accomplishment. May this milestone be a stepping stone toward even greater achievements, continued innovation and an even stronger global presence.

With best wishes!

Legacy of Leadership

Since 1955, EEPC India has been the face of Indian engineering exports. From a modest $10 million in the 1950s to over $116 billion in 2024–25, EEPC India has empowered 9,500+ members—60% from the MSME sector—through trade facilitation, policy advocacy, and global outreach.

Platinum Jubilee Celebration

The Platinum Jubilee was launched on 6th November 2024 by Hon’ble Minister Shri Piyush Goyal. The grand finale will be held on 8th September 2025, graced by the Hon’ble President of India, Smt. Droupadi Murmu. This milestone marks 70 years of innovation, collaboration, and global impact.

Vision 2030 & Beyond

The Government of India has set an ambitious target of US$ 2 trillion in exports by 2030, with US$ 1 trillion each from merchandise and services. Engineering exports are central to this vision, with EEPC India aiming for:

Ambitious Export Goals

  • US$ 250 billion in engineering exports by 2030
  • US$ 1.25 trillion by 2047, marking India’s 100 years of independence

Global Contribution

  • Increase India’s share in global engineering exports from 1% to 2.5–3% by 2030
  • Further to 8–10% by 2047

Comprehensive Value Chain

  • Promote high-value, advanced engineering products
  • Enhance global competitiveness across the entire value chain

Clean Commute Commitment

  • Support the transition to electric vehicles and clean transportation

Eco-friendly Leadership

  • Champion green energy and sustainable practices
  • Position India as a global leader in responsible engineering
